SprayCan

1 · Image

Load with Open image or Sample in the toolbar above. High-contrast subjects (portraits, logos, bold shapes) work best; your image is processed locally and never uploaded. (Remove background downloads a small model the first time.)

2 · Adjust

Invert, flip, and auto-contrast are in the toolbar above.

3 · Layers

Higher = cleaner: drops tiny specks and fills small islands, so you get a few clean bridges instead of hundreds.

Turn on Edge layer (toolbar) to add a top layer of strong outlines — a subject’s contour, bold creases — that the tonal layers miss; higher = more lines. Keep highlights (toolbar) protects eyes/nose/teeth.

4 · Bridges

Tiny islands are filled automatically; only the big ones get a tie. Click a tie to select it, then drag its handles or Delete it. Use “+ Add tie” to draw your own. Any island that still floats shows in red.

What are bridges?

A bridge is a thin sliver of material you leave uncut, to hold a “floating” piece in place — like the middle of an O or the dot of an i. Without it, that piece falls out the moment you finish cutting. SprayCan finds these islands (shown in red) and ties the big ones automatically; you can move, add, or delete each tie. They’re about the physical cut, not the printing.
